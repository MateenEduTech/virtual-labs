📱01Getting Started & Installation

The Virtual Science Lab is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — it can be installed directly from your browser onto your phone or computer, without visiting any app store. Once installed, it works completely offline.

Step 1 — Open in Browser
Open the app link in Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge on your Android phone or computer. Other browsers (Firefox, Safari) may have limited PWA support.
Step 2 — Install Prompt
After a few seconds, a banner appears at the bottom of the screen saying "Install Virtual Science Lab." Tap the "INSTALL" button. On desktop Chrome, look for the install icon in the address bar.
Step 3 — Add to Home Screen
The app icon appears on your home screen or desktop like a native app. Tap it to open — it loads instantly with no internet required.
Step 4 — Offline Use
All simulations, quizzes, notebooks, and content work entirely offline. Your notes and scores are saved on the device and persist between sessions.
🌐 For Teachers: Download the app on the classroom projector/laptop once with internet, then use it completely offline in class — no internet connection is needed for any subsequent use.

The app also supports manual installation as a local folder. Save the files (index.html, manifest.json, service-worker.js, author.jpg) in one folder and open index.html in a browser, or use VS Code Live Server for local hosting.

⚡03Physics Lab – Complete Guide

The Physics Lab contains four experiments. Select any experiment from the tabs at the top of the lab page.

🕰️ Simple Pendulum

Demonstrates Simple Harmonic Motion. Formula: T = 2π√(L/g). Adjust Length (0.2–2.0m), Gravity (1.6–25 m/s²) (try Moon gravity = 1.6!), and Amplitude (5–80°). Watch the bob swing and observe how period and frequency change in real time.

📐 Inclined Plane

Demonstrates Newton's Second Law. Formula: a = g·sin(θ). Use the Amplitude slider as the angle. Observe the block on the incline and the calculated acceleration. Change the angle to see how steeper inclines produce greater acceleration.

🔦 Light Refraction

Demonstrates Snell's Law. Formula: n₁·sin(θ₁) = n₂·sin(θ₂). Adjust the angle of incidence using the Amplitude slider. Observe the incident ray (yellow) and refracted ray (green) changing direction at the air-glass boundary.

💡 Electric Circuit

Demonstrates Ohm's Law: V=IR. The Length slider controls Voltage, the Gravity slider controls Resistance. Watch electrons flow through the circuit and observe how the bulb brightens as current increases. Live readings show V, R, I (current), and P (power).

🔬 NCF 2023 Inquiry Suggestion: For the pendulum experiment, ask students: "What happens to the period if we're on the Moon (g=1.6) vs Earth (g=9.8)? What about Jupiter (g=24.8)?" This develops comparative scientific reasoning.
🧪04Chemistry Lab – Complete Guide

The Chemistry Lab provides four safe virtual experiments. No real chemicals are used — all reactions are simulated with full visual feedback.

🔴 Acid-Base Reaction (Neutralization)

Mix HCl (acid) and NaOH (base) virtually. Adjust Acid Volume (0–100 mL) and Base Volume (0–100 mL). Observe the pH value change on the pH scale bar (0=strongly acidic, 7=neutral, 14=strongly basic), the indicator color shift (red→green→blue), and the heat released. The key discovery: equal volumes of equal-concentration acid and base produce neutral pH 7.

⚡ Electrolysis

Simulates the electrolysis of water: 2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂. Observe bubbles forming at electrodes. Change temperature to see how it affects reaction rate.

🔥 Combustion

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + Heat. Observe the exothermic reaction process and how temperature affects the reaction rate and product formation.

🧫 Titration

A quantitative experiment using M₁V₁ = M₂V₂ at the equivalence point. Observe how slowly adding base to acid reaches the exact neutralization point detected by indicator color change.

🌡️ Temperature Effect: Use the Temperature slider across all experiments to observe how higher temperatures affect reaction rates. This is a key NCF 2023 inquiry point — "How does temperature affect chemical reactions?"
🔬05Biology Lab – Complete Guide

The Biology Lab simulates biological processes and structures through interactive animations aligned with NCF 2023 biology curriculum.

🦠 Cell Microscopy

Simulates looking at plant cells under a microscope. Three sliders control the experience: Magnification (40x–2000x) — at higher magnification, see more detail but fewer cells; Focus (0–100%) — find the sweet spot around 50–65% for best clarity (simulates real focus adjustment); Stain Intensity (0–100%) — higher stain makes cell walls and nuclei more visible. At 400x+, chloroplasts become visible in green.

🌿 Photosynthesis

Visual simulation of photosynthesis: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂. Observe oxygen bubbles being produced by chloroplasts. Change light intensity using the Stain slider (simulating light) to see how production rate changes.

💧 Osmosis

Observe water molecules moving across a semi-permeable membrane. Change solute concentration using sliders and observe direction of net water movement — from low to high solute concentration.

🔬 Enzyme Activity

Simulate enzyme-substrate interactions. Observe how changing temperature and pH affect enzyme activity rates — showing optimal conditions and denaturation.

🔭 Magnification Guide: 40x–100x = Full cells visible | 200x = Cell boundaries clear | 400x = Nucleus visible | 1000x+ = Chloroplasts and fine structures. This mirrors real microscopy technique as required by NCERT practicals.
🚀06Conducting Simulations – Step by Step

Every simulation follows this structure. Use this as a classroom procedure or self-study guide:

Step 1 — Select Experiment
Go to the relevant lab (Physics, Chemistry, or Biology). Select your experiment from the tabs at the top.
Step 2 — Read the Concept Panel
Before starting, read the yellow Concept Panel which explains the scientific principle, the relevant formula, and the NCF 2023 / NEP 2020 connection. This is your "pre-lab reading."
Step 3 — Write a Hypothesis (Notebook)
Open the Notebook section → Hypothesis tab. Write what you predict will happen. Example: "If I increase the pendulum length, the time period will increase because L is in the numerator of √(L/g)."
Step 4 — Set Initial Conditions
Use the Control Panel sliders to set your starting values. Keep one variable at a time constant while changing only ONE variable — this is controlled experimentation as per scientific method.
Step 5 — Press START / REACT / VIEW
Press the green button to begin the simulation. Watch the animation and the Live Observations panel carefully.
Step 6 — Change Variables and Observe
While the simulation runs (or after pausing), change one slider and observe how the readings change. This is the core inquiry learning activity aligned with NCF 2023.
Step 7 — Record Observations (Notebook)
Open Notebook → Observations. Write down the readings for at least 3-4 different variable settings. Example: "L=0.5m, T=1.42s | L=1.0m, T=2.01s | L=2.0m, T=2.84s"
Step 8 — Use PAUSE & RESET
Press ⏸ PAUSE to freeze the simulation and read values carefully. Press ↺ RESET to start fresh with new variable settings.
Step 9 — Write Conclusion (Notebook)
Open Notebook → Conclusion. Compare your observations with your hypothesis. Explain whether the hypothesis was confirmed or rejected and why.
Step 10 — Take the Quiz
Scroll to the quiz at the bottom of the lab page. Answer the 3 questions to test your conceptual understanding. Review wrong answers to reinforce learning.
📓07Lab Notebook – How to Use

The Digital Lab Notebook is aligned with NEP 2020's emphasis on scientific documentation and the NCERT requirement for maintaining lab records. It has three tabs:

  • 💡 Hypothesis Tab — Write your prediction BEFORE conducting the experiment. A good hypothesis follows the format: "If [variable] is changed, then [outcome] will happen because [scientific reasoning]."
  • 👁️ Observations Tab — Record numerical readings and qualitative observations DURING the experiment. Be specific — include variable values and corresponding readings.
  • ✅ Conclusion Tab — Write your analysis AFTER the experiment. Compare observations to the hypothesis. State whether results confirm or contradict the prediction and why.

Press 💾 SAVE after writing in each tab. Entries are saved to the device using LocalStorage and persist between sessions — your notes are never lost.

The Notebook section also shows your Progress Bars for each lab and your earned Achievement Badges.

📋 For Teachers: Ask students to type their entries in the notebook and screenshot them as their digital lab report submission. This directly fulfills the PARAKH competency-based assessment model.
📝08Quizzes & Assessment

Each lab has a 3-question quiz at the bottom of the page. These are aligned with PARAKH's competency-based assessment model — questions test conceptual understanding, not rote memorization.

  • Read each question carefully — they are conceptual, not definitional
  • Select an answer option — you'll receive green feedback for correct, red for incorrect
  • The correct answer is always highlighted even if wrong, so you learn from mistakes
  • Your score is saved on the device and tracked in the Notebook section
  • Press ↺ RETRY after completing all questions to attempt again
💡 Teaching Strategy: Have students conduct the experiment FIRST, then take the quiz. Scores are typically higher after hands-on virtual experimentation — demonstrating the NEP 2020 principle that experiential learning improves conceptual understanding.

👨‍🏫10Teacher's Guide

This app is designed as a classroom teaching tool aligned with NEP 2020 and NCF 2023 pedagogical frameworks. Here's how to integrate it effectively:

  • Pre-class (Flipped Learning, NEP 2020) — Assign students to explore a specific experiment at home and write a hypothesis in the notebook before the class discussion
  • In-class Demonstration — Project the app on screen and demonstrate experiments while asking students to predict outcomes before revealing results (inquiry-based, NCF 2023)
  • Controlled Variable Activity — Ask each student group to keep all variables constant and change only ONE slider. Compare results across groups to teach controlled experimentation
  • Lab Report Assignment (PARAKH) — Require students to complete all three notebook tabs (hypothesis, observations, conclusion) and submit screenshots as their digital lab report
  • Cross-Lab Inquiry — Ask questions like "How is the concept of variables in the pendulum experiment similar to how we change acid volume in chemistry?" (connecting labs, NCF 2023 cross-disciplinary thinking)
  • Quiz Assessment — Use quiz scores as formative assessment. Students who score 2/3 or below should redo the experiment with guidance before retaking the quiz
📱 No-Internet Classroom: The app works fully offline. Install it once on the school computer or projector and use it in classes indefinitely — no internet required. Students can also install it on personal phones for home study.
🔬11Scientific Method Guide

All simulations are designed around the Scientific Method as prescribed by NCERT and emphasized in NEP 2020 and NCF 2023:

1
OBSERVE
Read the Concept Panel. Understand the phenomenon. Look at the initial simulation state before pressing START.
2
QUESTION
What will happen if I change variable X? What is the relationship between variable A and outcome B?
3
HYPOTHESIZE
Write a testable prediction in the Notebook → Hypothesis tab. Use "If...then...because..." format.
4
EXPERIMENT
Run the simulation. Change ONE variable at a time. Press START, adjust sliders, observe the animation and live readings.
5
RECORD DATA
Write numerical observations in Notebook → Observations. Record at least 3 data points for each variable change.
6
ANALYZE
Look for patterns in your data. Does increasing X always increase Y? Is there a formula that fits your data?
7
CONCLUDE
Write conclusions in Notebook → Conclusion. Was your hypothesis correct? What does the data show? What would you investigate next?
