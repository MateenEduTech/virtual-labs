📐 Concept – Simple Pendulum

A simple pendulum exhibits Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM). Its period depends on the length of the string and gravitational acceleration.

T = 2π√(L/g)

As per NCF 2023, students learn by manipulating variables — change length or gravity and observe how the period changes. This develops scientific reasoning as emphasized in NEP 2020.

🦠 Concept – Cell Structure

The cell is the fundamental unit of life. Plant cells have a cell wall, chloroplasts, and a large vacuole. Animal cells lack these but have lysosomes and centrioles.

Plant Cell: Cell Wall + Chloroplast + Vacuole | Animal Cell: Lysosome + Centriole

As per NCF 2023, observation-based learning deepens understanding of cellular biology. NEP 2020 encourages use of digital microscopy tools for inquiry-based learning.

About Virtual Science Lab

A comprehensive digital solution for science education in resource-limited schools — built on NEP 2020, NCF 2023, PARAKH, and NCERT frameworks.

Deeply committed to bridging the digital divide in science education, he has conceptualized a suite of NEP 2020-aligned digital learning tools to ensure every student — regardless of school infrastructure — has access to quality, hands-on science education. This Virtual Science Lab is part of a larger vision to create a national digital education ecosystem aligned with India's education reform goals.

POLICY ALIGNMENT

NEP 2020 NCF 2023 PARAKH NCERT

National Education Policy 2020 strongly emphasizes experiential learning, scientific temper, and technology integration. This app directly addresses NEP 2020's vision by providing interactive, inquiry-based science learning to every student.


National Curriculum Framework 2023 stresses activity-based science learning and conceptual understanding through observation. Every simulation in this lab is designed to align with NCF's pedagogical philosophy.


PARAKH (Performance Assessment, Review, and Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development) evaluates conceptual understanding and experimental reasoning. This lab builds exactly these competencies.

THE PROBLEM THIS SOLVES

  • Thousands of schools across India lack proper laboratory infrastructure
  • Students memorize experiments instead of performing them
  • Safety concerns prevent access to certain chemical experiments
  • Shortage of trained lab assistants in rural and semi-urban schools
  • Science education becomes purely theoretical without practical exposure

The Virtual Science Lab provides a safe, interactive, offline digital environment where students can conduct experiments, manipulate variables, and understand scientific principles through virtual experimentation — without any physical infrastructure requirement.

EDUCATIONAL THEORY

  • Experiential Learning Theory – Students learn by doing and observing
  • Inquiry-Based Learning – Students form and test hypotheses
  • Constructivist Theory – Knowledge built through active interaction
  • Scientific Method Learning – Hypothesis → Experiment → Conclusion

TECHNOLOGY

  • Progressive Web App (PWA) – Works offline, installs like a native app
  • Built with HTML, CSS, JavaScript – No dependencies, no servers
  • LocalStorage – Student progress saved on device
  • Canvas API – Real-time physics/chemistry/biology simulations
  • Service Worker – Full offline caching
  • Installable on Android and desktop via browser

01 — GETTING STARTED & INSTALLATION

The Virtual Science Lab is a Progressive Web App (PWA) that can be installed directly on your Android phone or desktop computer without visiting any app store.

STEP 1
Open the app link in Chrome or Edge browser on your device.
STEP 2
You will see an "Install App" banner at the bottom of the screen. Tap "Install Now".
STEP 3
The app installs on your home screen like a native app.
STEP 4
Once installed, the app works fully offline — no internet needed.

Offline use: All simulations, quizzes, and the notebook function entirely without internet after first load.

03 — PHYSICS LAB – HOW TO USE

The Physics Lab contains four experiments: Simple Pendulum, Inclined Plane, Light Refraction, and Electric Circuit.

  1. Select an experiment from the tabs at the top of the lab page.
  2. Read the Concept Panel — it explains the scientific principle and relevant formula.
  3. Use the Control Panel sliders to set variables (e.g., length, gravity, amplitude for a pendulum).
  4. Press ▶ START to begin the animation.
  5. Watch the Live Observations panel — readings update in real time.
  6. Change a variable and observe how readings change — this is inquiry-based learning as per NCF 2023.
  7. Record your findings in the Notebook.
  8. Press ⏸ PAUSE to freeze simulation; ↺ RESET to restart.

04 — CHEMISTRY LAB – HOW TO USE

Experiments: Acid-Base Reaction, Electrolysis, Combustion, Titration.

  1. Select an experiment from the tabs.
  2. Read the Concept Panel to understand the chemical reaction.
  3. Adjust Acid Volume, Base Volume, and Temperature sliders.
  4. Press ▶ REACT to start the simulation.
  5. Observe pH changes, indicator color changes, bubble formation, and heat values in the Live Observations panel.
  6. Change acid/base ratio and note how pH shifts — from acidic (red) to neutral (green) to basic (blue).
  7. Record observations in the Notebook.

05 — BIOLOGY LAB – HOW TO USE

Experiments: Cell Microscopy, Photosynthesis, Osmosis, Enzyme Activity.

  1. Select an experiment.
  2. Read the Concept Panel to understand the biological system being studied.
  3. Adjust Magnification, Focus, and Stain Intensity.
  4. Press ▶ VIEW to start the simulation.
  5. Observe the animated biological structures — cells, organelles, processes.
  6. At different magnifications, different details become visible — simulating real microscopy.
  7. Note cell count, organelle visibility, and clarity in your Notebook.

06 — LAB NOTEBOOK

The Lab Notebook (aligned with NEP 2020 documentation skills) has three tabs:

  • Hypothesis – Write your prediction before the experiment
  • Observations – Record what you saw during the experiment
  • Conclusion – Write what the experiment proved or disproved

Press 💾 SAVE after writing. All entries are saved automatically on your device using LocalStorage and persist between sessions.

07 — CONDUCTING SIMULATIONS (STEP-BY-STEP)

Follow the Scientific Method as per NEP 2020 & NCF 2023:

STEP 1 – OBSERVE
Read the Concept Panel. Note the formula and key principle.
STEP 2 – HYPOTHESIZE
Open Notebook → Hypothesis tab. Write what you predict will happen when you change a variable.
STEP 3 – EXPERIMENT
Adjust one variable at a time using sliders. Press START. Observe live readings.
STEP 4 – RECORD
Open Notebook → Observations. Write down the readings for each variable setting.
STEP 5 – CONCLUDE
Open Notebook → Conclusion. Compare results to your hypothesis. Write final analysis.
STEP 6 – QUIZ
Answer the quiz at the bottom of each lab to test understanding.

08 — QUIZZES & ASSESSMENT

Each lab includes a 3-question quiz aligned with PARAKH's competency-based assessment model.

  • Questions test conceptual understanding, not rote memorization
  • Select an answer and receive instant feedback (green = correct, red = incorrect)
  • Scores are saved locally and tracked in the Notebook section
  • Teachers can ask students to screenshot their quiz scores

09 — TEACHER'S GUIDE

This app is designed to support classroom science instruction aligned with NEP 2020:

  • Pre-class: Assign specific experiments for students to explore at home
  • In-class: Use on projector/screen to demonstrate experiments before discussion
  • Assessment: Ask students to submit Notebook entries as lab reports
  • Inquiry: Pose questions like "What happens if you change gravity to Moon's value (1.6)?
  • Offline use: Students can use the app in areas with no internet
